# Stakeholder Identification for Ticket Triage Automation

## Primary Stakeholders (Required for Demo)

### 1. Support Team Leadership
**Role**: Validate classification rules and routing logic
**Impact**: Direct users of the automation
**Feedback needed on**:
- Ticket categorization accuracy
- Priority assignment logic
- Routing suggestions (tier1 vs tier2)
- Edge case handling

**Suggested representatives**:
- Head of Customer Support
- Support Team Manager
- Senior Support Agents (2-3)

### 2. Product Management
**Role**: Provide business context and feature prioritization
**Impact**: Align automation with product roadmap
**Feedback needed on**:
- Feature request handling
- Bug vs feature classification
- Integration with product development workflow

**Suggested representatives**:
- Product Manager
- Product Owner

### 3. Engineering Leadership
**Role**: Review technical architecture and scalability
**Impact**: Ensure production readiness
**Feedback needed on**:
- Technical architecture
- Scalability requirements
- Integration points
- Monitoring and alerting

**Suggested representatives**:
- Engineering Manager
- DevOps/SRE Lead

### 4. Customer Success
**Role**: Assess impact on customer experience
**Impact**: Ensure automation improves customer satisfaction
**Feedback needed on**:
- Response time improvements
- Customer satisfaction metrics
- Escalation procedures

**Suggested representatives**:
- Head of Customer Success
- Customer Success Manager

## Secondary Stakeholders (Optional for Demo)

### 5. Sales & Marketing
**Role**: Understand automation capabilities for customer pitches
**Impact**: Business development and sales enablement

### 6. Executive Leadership
**Role**: Strategic alignment and investment decisions
**Impact**: Budget approval and strategic direction

## Stakeholder Engagement Plan

### Phase 1: Initial Demo (Week 1)
**Participants**: Primary stakeholders (groups 1-4)
**Format**: 30-minute demo + 15-minute Q&A
**Goal**: Collect initial feedback and validate approach

### Phase 2: Deep Dive Sessions (Week 2)
**Participants**: Individual stakeholder groups
**Format**: 60-minute working sessions
**Goal**: Detailed feedback and requirements gathering

### Phase 3: Pilot Planning (Week 3)
**Participants**: Cross-functional team
**Format**: 90-minute planning session
**Goal**: Define pilot scope, success metrics, and rollout plan

## Communication Plan

### Pre-Demo Communication
1. **Email invitation** with demo agenda and objectives
2. **Background materials** sent 24 hours before demo:
   - Prototype overview (1-page summary)
   - Sample classification output
   - Success metrics and business impact

### During Demo
1. **Live demonstration** of prototype functionality
2. **Interactive Q&A** session
3. **Feedback collection** via structured questions

### Post-Demo Follow-up
1. **Summary email** with key decisions and next steps
2. **Feedback consolidation** document
3. **Action items** assigned with owners and deadlines

## Success Criteria for Stakeholder Engagement

### Quantitative Metrics
- **Attendance**: > 80% of invited primary stakeholders
- **Feedback quality**: > 90% of stakeholders provide actionable feedback
- **Alignment**: > 85% agreement on classification rules and priorities

### Qualitative Metrics
- **Stakeholder satisfaction**: Positive feedback on demo clarity and relevance
- **Decision clarity**: Clear understanding of next steps and timeline
- **Engagement level**: Active participation in Q&A and feedback sessions

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Low stakeholder attendance
**Mitigation**: Schedule multiple demo sessions at different times
**Mitigation**: Record demo for stakeholders who cannot attend

### Risk: Conflicting feedback
**Mitigation**: Use structured feedback forms with clear categories
**Mitigation**: Facilitate discussion to resolve conflicts during demo

### Risk: Unclear requirements
**Mitigation**: Provide concrete examples and use cases
**Mitigation**: Follow up with one-on-one clarification sessions
